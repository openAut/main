# Example: Heat Pump with Low Efficiency

## Situation
A single‑family house with an air‑to‑water heat pump complains of high electricity bills and insufficient heating when outdoor temperatures drop below 0 °C. The heat pump is five years old, and maintenance has been minimal. The system includes floor heating and a domestic hot water tank.

## Likely causes
1. **Dirty outdoor coil** – accumulation of dirt or ice reduces heat transfer and COP.
2. **Insufficient airflow or blocked filters** – restricted airflow on the indoor or outdoor side lowers efficiency.
3. **Refrigerant leak or incorrect charge** – low refrigerant reduces heating capacity; only certified technicians can diagnose and repair.
4. **Inadequate sizing or installation** – heat pump may be undersized or not optimally located.
5. **Improper control settings** – incorrect supply temperature setpoint or sensor placement can cause frequent defrost cycles or short cycling.

## Controls and measurements
- **Inspect and clean coils and filters**; remove debris and ensure water drains freely.
- **Measure supply and return temperatures** of the hydronic loop; calculate COP (heat output ÷ electrical input).
- **Check outdoor unit location**: Ensure adequate clearance for airflow; verify that snow or vegetation does not block intake/exhaust.
- **Monitor operating hours and defrost cycles**: Frequent defrost indicates excessive frosting.
- **Verify refrigerant pressure and charge**: Requires certified HVAC technician; do not attempt without qualification.
- **Review control settings**: Check setpoints for supply water temperature, weather compensation curves, and defrost settings.

## Recommended actions
1. Clean outdoor and indoor coils; replace or clean filters.
2. Remove obstructions around outdoor unit.
3. Adjust supply temperature setpoints and activate weather compensation to match outdoor temperatures.
4. Schedule a certified technician to check refrigerant charge, compressor performance, and defrost operation.
5. Consider adding supplemental heating or insulation if the heat pump is undersized for extreme cold.

## Energy impact
High: A poorly functioning heat pump can have a low COP, leading to high energy consumption. Regular cleaning and maintenance can restore efficiency.

## Regulation and compliance notes
- Work on refrigerants must be performed by certified personnel to comply with safety and environmental regulations.
- Ensure heat pump operation meets energy declaration and building code requirements.

## Risks and assumptions
- Ice removal must be done carefully to avoid damaging coils.
- Refrigerant leaks pose environmental and safety risks; only professionals should handle them.
- If the unit is undersized, replacement may be required.

## Questions for precision
- When was the heat pump last serviced?
- What is the design heating load of the house?
- Are there any error codes displayed on the heat pump controller?
- Does the system include a backup heater?
