# Commissioning and Start‑up

Commissioning ensures that HVAC systems are installed, balanced, and operate according to design intent. Proper commissioning reduces energy use, improves comfort, and facilitates troubleshooting.

## Commissioning phases
1. **Design review**: Verify that design documents meet performance requirements; check integration of ventilation, heating, cooling, and controls; identify testable performance criteria.
2. **Pre‑installation inspection**: Inspect equipment and materials on delivery; verify that ductwork and piping are correctly sized and sealed; check that sensors, valves, and dampers are properly located.
3. **Installation verification**: Confirm that fans, pumps, coils, and heat recovery units are installed according to drawings; ensure that electrical wiring and control cables are labelled and connected.
4. **Start‑up**: Energise equipment, check rotation direction of fans and pumps, verify setpoints, and ensure that safety devices (pressure switches, frost protection) function.
5. **Balancing**: Measure and adjust air and water flows to achieve design values; record fan and pump curves; adjust balancing valves and dampers.
6. **Functional performance testing**: Test control sequences under various load conditions, including occupancy modes, night setback, free cooling, and alarms; verify VAV box operation and BMS integration.
7. **Documentation and training**: Provide operation and maintenance manuals, as-built drawings, and training for facility staff.

## Continuous commissioning
After initial commissioning, continuous commissioning uses monitoring and analytics to detect performance drift and identify optimisation opportunities. BMS trend data should be reviewed regularly to adjust schedules, setpoints, and equipment settings.

## Measurement and verification
Key measurements during commissioning include:
- Airflow (supply, return, exhaust).
- Static pressure across fans and filters.
- Temperature and humidity at supply and return.
- Water flow in hydronic circuits.
- Control signals and actuator positions.
- Power consumption of fans, pumps, chillers, and boilers.
Documentation of measurements provides a baseline for future comparisons.

## Commissioning benefits
- Confirms that systems meet comfort and energy requirements.
- Identifies installation errors or equipment faults early.
- Provides baseline data for troubleshooting and optimisation.
- Supports compliance with regulatory requirements and certification schemes (e.g., OVK, energy declarations).
