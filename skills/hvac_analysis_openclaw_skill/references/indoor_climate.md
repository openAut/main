# Indoor Climate Parameters

Indoor climate affects occupant comfort, health, and productivity. Key parameters include temperature, humidity, CO₂, draught, noise, and odours.

## Temperature
- **Operative temperature** combines air temperature and mean radiant temperature, reflecting perceived warmth.
- Comfort ranges depend on season and clothing; typical comfort band is 20–24 °C in winter and 22–26 °C in summer.
- Uneven temperatures can indicate imbalance in heating/cooling distribution or faulty controls.

## Humidity
- Relative humidity (RH) expresses moisture content as a percentage of the maximum at a given temperature. Folkhälsomyndigheten states that RH indoors during the heating season is typically **20–40 %**, with an average around **30 %**(FoHMFS 2014:18, avsnitt fukt).
- Absolute humidity difference above **3 g/m³** between indoor and outdoor air suggests poor ventilation(FoHMFS 2014:18, avsnitt fukt).
- Too low RH (<20 %) may cause dry eyes and throat; too high RH (>60 %) may promote mould growth.

## CO₂ concentration
- CO₂ is an indicator of human occupancy and ventilation performance.
- Outdoor CO₂ ≈ 400 ppm; well‑ventilated buildings typically maintain 600–800 ppm; values above **1 000 ppm** indicate insufficient ventilation(FoHMFS 2014:18, avsnitt CO₂).
- Elevated CO₂ can cause drowsiness and reduced cognitive performance.

## Draught
- Draught is unwanted local cooling caused by excessive air movement. It is influenced by air velocity, turbulence, and air temperature.
- For comfort, air velocity should be below 0.15–0.2 m/s in occupied zones; draught risk increases at lower temperatures.

## Noise
- HVAC systems can produce noise via fans, airflow in ducts, or vibrations.
- Acceptable noise levels vary by space type (e.g., <30 dB(A) in bedrooms, <35 dB(A) in offices).
- Excessive noise may signal high airflow, unbalanced fans, or loose components.

## Odours
- Unpleasant odours indicate contamination, microbial growth, or inadequate ventilation.
- Investigate sources (drains, garbage, materials) and ensure adequate exhaust.

## Monitoring and measurement
- Use data loggers or BMS sensors to monitor temperature, RH, CO₂, and noise.
- Compare measurements to design values and comfort guidelines.
- Investigate anomalies promptly.
