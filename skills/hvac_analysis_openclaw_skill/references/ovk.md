# Obligatory Ventilation Control (OVK)

OVK (**Obligatorisk ventilationskontroll**) is a mandatory Swedish inspection to ensure that ventilation systems function properly and provide sufficient fresh air. This summary highlights key requirements and intervals.

## Purpose
- Verify that ventilation systems deliver designed airflows.
- Ensure that systems are clean and well maintained.
- Confirm that instructions for operation and maintenance are available.
- Identify energy‑saving improvements without compromising indoor air quality.

## Inspection intervals
Inspection frequency depends on system type and building use(PBF 2011:338):

| Building/System type                        | Interval |
|--------------------------------------------|---------|
| Day-care centres, schools, healthcare      | 3 years |
| Multi‑dwelling buildings with FT/FTX or offices with supply and extract systems | 3 years |
| Multi‑dwelling buildings or offices with exhaust‑only (F) | 6 years |
| Single-family houses with supply and extract systems (FT/FTX) | Initial inspection only |

## Inspection procedure
1. **Document review**: The inspector checks drawings, design data, and previous inspection reports.
2. **Function tests**: Measurements of supply and exhaust flows, pressure balance, heat recovery efficiency, and control functions.
3. **Cleanliness check**: Inspect ducts, filters, heat exchangers, and terminal devices for dust, grease, or microbial growth.
4. **User information**: Verify that building owners and occupants receive instructions on operating ventilation systems.
5. **Reporting**: The inspector issues a certificate; deficiencies must be corrected and re‑inspected.

## Responsibilities
- **Building owner**: Responsible for scheduling OVK, carrying out necessary maintenance, and keeping records.
- **Certified inspector**: Performs the inspection and reports results.
- **Authorities**: Municipalities supervise compliance; non‑compliance may lead to orders or penalties.

Regular OVK helps maintain healthy indoor environments and ensures energy is not wasted due to poor ventilation performance.
