# Troubleshooting HVAC Systems

This guide outlines a systematic approach to diagnosing and resolving HVAC problems.

## General approach
1. **Define the symptom**: Gather detailed information about the problem (what, where, when, who is affected).
2. **Collect data**: Measure relevant parameters (airflow, temperature, humidity, pressure, CO₂, energy consumption). Use BMS trends if available.
3. **Check simple issues first**: Ensure that filters are clean, fans/pumps operate, dampers and valves are not stuck, and thermostats or sensors are calibrated.
4. **Identify root causes**: Separate symptoms from underlying causes (e.g., cold rooms due to airflow imbalance rather than insufficient heating capacity).
5. **Prioritise health and safety**: Address any risks (e.g., refrigerant leaks, mould, legionella) before proceeding with optimisation.
6. **Develop corrective actions**: Recommend operational changes, maintenance, balancing, control adjustments, or equipment repair/replacement.
7. **Evaluate energy and compliance impacts**: Consider how each recommendation affects energy use and whether it meets regulations.
8. **Document findings**: Record measurements, analysis, and actions for future reference.

## Common issues by symptom
- **Low airflow**: Dirty filters, blocked vents or ducts, malfunctioning fans, closed dampers, incorrect balancing.
- **High CO₂ levels**: Insufficient outdoor air supply, high occupancy not accounted for, faulty demand control sensors.
- **Temperature imbalance**: Uneven hydronic flow, defective thermostats, unbalanced VAV boxes, insulation problems.
- **High energy use**: Continuous operation of fans/pumps, simultaneous heating and cooling, poor heat recovery, leaks in ducts or hydronic system, oversized equipment.
- **Noise**: High airflow velocity, unbalanced fans, loose components, cavitation in pumps, water hammer.
- **Odours/mould**: Standing water in ducts or drains, contaminated filters or coils, inadequate exhaust, high humidity.

## Diagnostic tools
- Anemometers and flow hoods for measuring airflow.
- Manometers and differential pressure gauges.
- Temperature and humidity loggers.
- CO₂ monitors.
- Infrared thermography for detecting heat loss.
- BMS trend data and alarm logs.

## Follow‑up
After implementing corrective actions, monitor the system to verify improvement. Adjust recommendations based on feedback and new measurements.
