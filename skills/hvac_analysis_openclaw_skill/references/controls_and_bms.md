# Controls and Building Management Systems (BMS)

Modern HVAC systems rely on control systems to maintain comfort and efficiency. Building Management Systems (BMS) integrate sensors, actuators, and control logic to manage HVAC equipment, lighting, and other systems.

## Sensors
Common sensors include temperature, humidity, CO₂, pressure, airflow, and occupancy. Accurate sensors are essential; drift or failure can lead to poor control. Calibration should be performed regularly.

## Actuators
Actuators include valves, dampers, variable-frequency drives (VFDs) on fans and pumps, and compressor stages. They modulate flow, temperature, or pressure according to control signals.

## Control strategies
- **On/off control:** Simple on/off operation based on setpoint; may cause short cycling and wear.
- **Proportional–integral–derivative (PID):** Adjusts output proportionally and with integration and derivative actions to maintain setpoints with minimal overshoot.
- **Schedule control:** Enables/disables equipment based on time of day and occupancy.
- **Demand control:** Modulates ventilation based on CO₂ or VOC sensors, adjusting VAV dampers or fan speeds.
- **Night setback:** Allows temperature drift during unoccupied periods to save energy.

## Maintenance and troubleshooting
- Review alarm and trend logs to identify anomalies.
- Check that setpoints and schedules reflect actual usage.
- Ensure sensors are calibrated and not obstructed.
- Verify that dampers and valves respond correctly.
- Update control logic and software as necessary.
