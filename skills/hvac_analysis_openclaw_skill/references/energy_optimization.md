# Energy Optimisation for HVAC Systems

Energy optimisation reduces operating costs and environmental impact while maintaining comfort. The following strategies draw from industry practice and energy-efficiency guidance(Energimyndigheten ET 2016:02)(Energimyndigheten ET 2016:02).

## Conduct surveys and maintain equipment
- **Map flows and energy consumption**: perform a ventilation survey to identify over‑ventilation or under‑ventilation; measure flows and temperatures(Energimyndigheten ET 2016:02).
- **Clean regularly**: clean filters, ducts, fans, and heat exchangers; fouled components increase pressure drop and reduce heat recovery(Energimyndigheten ET 2016:02).
- **Service fans and motors**: lubricate bearings and replace worn parts to maintain efficiency(Energimyndigheten ET 2016:02).

## Adjust operation to demand
- **Schedule operation**: run fans and pumps only when needed; avoid 24/7 operation if not required(Energimyndigheten ET 2016:02).
- **Demand control**: use CO₂ or occupancy sensors to modulate VAV dampers or fan speeds, reducing airflow during low occupancy(Energimyndigheten ET 2016:02).
- **Night setbacks**: allow indoor temperature to drift within acceptable limits when unoccupied.

## Upgrade components
- **Install energy-efficient fans** and motors; select high‑efficiency models(Energimyndigheten ET 2016:02).
- **Use variable frequency drives (VFDs)** on fans and pumps to adjust speed according to demand; reduce speed during low load to save energy(Energimyndigheten ET 2016:02).
- **Calibrate thermostats and sensors** to avoid unnecessary operation(Energimyndigheten ET 2016:02).

## Optimise controls
- **Automatic control**: implement algorithms based on real‑time data (temperature, humidity, air quality) to adjust ventilation and heating/cooling(Energimyndigheten ET 2016:02).
- **Time control**: ensure ventilation runs only when needed; integrate schedules with building occupancy(Energimyndigheten ET 2016:02).
- **Chiller and boiler sequencing**: stage equipment to operate at high efficiency; avoid simultaneous heating and cooling.

## Recover and reuse energy
- **Heat recovery**: use heat exchangers (rotary, plate, or run‑around) to transfer heat from exhaust to supply air(Energimyndigheten ET 2016:02).
- **Cooling recovery/free cooling**: use outside air or water to cool the building when conditions permit(Energimyndigheten ET 2016:02).

## Reduce leaks and improve insulation
- **Seal duct leaks and improve airtightness** to prevent loss of conditioned air(Energimyndigheten ET 2016:02).
- **Insulate ducts and pipes** to minimise heat loss or gain(Energimyndigheten ET 2016:02).
- **Check building envelope** for air leaks around doors, windows, and penetrations.

Implementing these measures should be prioritised based on cost-effectiveness and ease of execution. Start with low‑cost operational adjustments and maintenance, then consider investments in equipment and control upgrades.
