# Energy Optimisation for HVAC Systems

Energy optimisation reduces operating costs and environmental impact while maintaining comfort. The following strategies draw from industry practice and energy-efficiency guidance【631875643103375†L119-L166】【631875643103375†L171-L232】.

## Conduct surveys and maintain equipment
- **Map flows and energy consumption**: perform a ventilation survey to identify over‑ventilation or under‑ventilation; measure flows and temperatures【631875643103375†L119-L131】.
- **Clean regularly**: clean filters, ducts, fans, and heat exchangers; fouled components increase pressure drop and reduce heat recovery【631875643103375†L134-L142】.
- **Service fans and motors**: lubricate bearings and replace worn parts to maintain efficiency【631875643103375†L119-L142】.

## Adjust operation to demand
- **Schedule operation**: run fans and pumps only when needed; avoid 24/7 operation if not required【631875643103375†L134-L142】.
- **Demand control**: use CO₂ or occupancy sensors to modulate VAV dampers or fan speeds, reducing airflow during low occupancy【631875643103375†L171-L181】.
- **Night setbacks**: allow indoor temperature to drift within acceptable limits when unoccupied.

## Upgrade components
- **Install energy-efficient fans** and motors; select high‑efficiency models【631875643103375†L146-L166】.
- **Use variable frequency drives (VFDs)** on fans and pumps to adjust speed according to demand; reduce speed during low load to save energy【631875643103375†L146-L166】.
- **Calibrate thermostats and sensors** to avoid unnecessary operation【631875643103375†L146-L166】.

## Optimise controls
- **Automatic control**: implement algorithms based on real‑time data (temperature, humidity, air quality) to adjust ventilation and heating/cooling【631875643103375†L171-L181】.
- **Time control**: ensure ventilation runs only when needed; integrate schedules with building occupancy【631875643103375†L171-L181】.
- **Chiller and boiler sequencing**: stage equipment to operate at high efficiency; avoid simultaneous heating and cooling.

## Recover and reuse energy
- **Heat recovery**: use heat exchangers (rotary, plate, or run‑around) to transfer heat from exhaust to supply air【631875643103375†L192-L202】.
- **Cooling recovery/free cooling**: use outside air or water to cool the building when conditions permit【631875643103375†L192-L202】.

## Reduce leaks and improve insulation
- **Seal duct leaks and improve airtightness** to prevent loss of conditioned air【631875643103375†L212-L232】.
- **Insulate ducts and pipes** to minimise heat loss or gain【631875643103375†L212-L232】.
- **Check building envelope** for air leaks around doors, windows, and penetrations.

Implementing these measures should be prioritised based on cost-effectiveness and ease of execution. Start with low‑cost operational adjustments and maintenance, then consider investments in equipment and control upgrades.
